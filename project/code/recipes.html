Mango Berry Smoothie

Overview: Smoothies are one of my favorite go-to breakfasts. They are very filling and a great way to incorporate fruit into your diet. I often find it difficult to buy fruit that isn’t frozen because it doesn’t stay fresh for a long time. You can make this smoothie with almost any combination of ingredients but I found the proportion of liquid to fruit to work very well for smoothie bowls.

Ingredients
  • 1/2 cup frozen mango
  • 1 banana
  • 1/2 cup raspberries
  • 1 cup milk or milk alternative
  • granola (optional)
Tips:

Start by adding a little bit of fruit and blending. If you want more smoothie or a thicker consistency you can slowly add more fruit or milk. It’s easy to accidentally make too much when the proportions are off.

Method
  1. Put ingredients in blender and mix at high speed until it reaches your desired consistency
  2. Either serve as a drink or in smoothie bowl and top it off with granola

Avocado/Egg Toast


Overview: This breakfast is quick to make and can be adapted to any ingredients you have available at the time. All you really need in egg, avocado, and toast but this version of the recipe has become my personal favorite.

Ingredients
  • Whole wheat sourdough bread
  • 1 Egg
  • 1/2 avocado
  • 3 slices tomato
  • Arugula
  • Balsamic glaze (optional)
  • Hot sauce (optional)
Method
  1. Toast slice of bread in toaster or toaster oven to desired amount
  2. Heat and oil a skillet on medium heat
  3. Fry egg until the edges are crispy and golden (around 4 minutes)
  4. Assemble toast: spread the mashed avocado, and add tomato slices, egg, and arugula on top
  5. Glaze with balsamic reduction or hot sauce

Banana Pancakes

Overview: Banana pancakes are such a simple and fun weekend option for when you’re craving something sweet but not too sweet. Regular pancakes are usually too sweet and bland for me but these banana pancakes always hit the spot.

Ingredients
  • 1 cup unbleached all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • pinch of salt
  • maple syrup (optional)
  • berries (optional)
Method
  1. Combine flour, white sugar, baking powder and salt
  2. In another bowl, mix together the egg, milk, vegetable oil and banana
  3. Stir the flour mixture into banana mixture
  4. Oil lightly and heat a frying pan over medium heat
  5. Pour ¼ cup of batter into pan at a time and cook until golden brown on both sides
  6. Serve with maple syrup and berries

Turkey Sandwich


Overview: I recently discovered how good sandwiches are and I came across a similar recipe online and loved it. This is my absolute favorite sandwich. The pesto and avocado work together very nicely to create a filling and nutritious breakfast/lunch.

Ingredients
  • 2 slices of turkey
  • Pesto
  • 2 Slices of Whole Wheat Sourdough Bread
  • 1/2 Tomato
  • 1/4 cup of Arugula
  • 1/2 Avocado
  • 1-3 slices of cheese
Method
  1. Toast the slices of whole wheat sourdough bread in a toaster or toaster oven to your liking
  2. Mash half of an avocado onto one piece of bread and spread pesto on the other slice
  3. Fill the sandwich with the remaining ingredients and enjoy

Chickpea Avocado Sandwich (Vegan)

Overview: This is another one of my favorite sandwiches and it's also a tasty vegan option. The chickpeas and avocado are a great source of nutrition and it’s also very quick to make and surprisingly filling.

Ingredients
  • 2 slices of Whole Wheat Sourdough Bread
  • 1/2 Avocado
  • 1/2 can of chickpeas
  • 1 tsp of olive oil
  • pinch of salt
  • pinch of pepper
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
Method
  1. Toast the slices of bread in a toaster or toaster oven to your desired amount
  2. Put the chickpeas into a medium sized bowl and mash them with the olive oil
  3. In a small bowl, mash the avocado and combine with lemon juice and pepper and salt to taste
  4. Spread the chickpeas on one side of the bread and the avocado on the other side

Personal Pan Pizza


Overview: This recipe is perfect for when you’re in a rush or want to eat right away. It’s very customizable and you can experiment with different toppings. I’ve found that pesto and olives also go very nicely with this recipe.

Ingredients
  • 1 large flour/corn tortilla
  • Tomato sauce
  • Shredded Cheese (mozarella recommended)
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese (finely sliced)
  • Arugula
  • Balsamic glaze (optional)
  • Other desired toppings
Method
  1. Heat medium/large skillet and place tortilla flat on the pan on low/medium heat
  2. Spread tomato sauce over the tortilla
  3. Put on the shredded cheese, mozzarella, and arugula
  4. When the cheese melts remove from heat and drizzle with balsamic glaze
Tips

Make sure to keep the heat on medium/low so that the tortilla doesn't burn

Chicken & Veggie Pasta


Overview: This has been one of my favorite dinner options lately. Sometimes I’ll make it with brown rice instead of pasta for a more healthy alternative. I recommend using the Trader Joe’s Heat & Serve Balsamic Vinegar and Rosemary Chicken if you don’t have the time to cook raw chicken for this recipe.

Ingredients
  • Trader Joe's pre-cooked rosemary seasoned chicken or 1 chicken breast
  • 1/2 cup of mushrooms
  • 1 Zucchini
  • 3/4 cup of pasta (Penne or Rigatoni recommended)
  • 2-3 tablespoons Olive Oil
  • Mushroom Umami seasoning
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Parmesan (optional)
Method
  1. Boil water in a saucepan for the pasta
  2. Chop the mushrooms, zucchini, and chicken into medium sized pieces
  3. Put pasta in boiling water
  4. Heat and oil a skillet
  5. Cook the mushrooms, zucchini, and chicken in the skillet and season with Trader Joe's mushroom company umami seasoning or any other seasoning of your choice
  6. Drain pasta and add to skillet
  7. Combine all the ingredients and add parmesan to taste

Spicy Penne Alla Vodka


Overview: This spicy penne alla vodka has been all over the internet lately but it is one of my personal favorite classics. It can be more or less spicy depending on the amount of pepper flakes you use. For a healthier option you can use whole wheat or chickpea pasta. I usually eat this dish with Brussels sprouts or a salad.

Ingredients
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1/4 cup of tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon vodka
  • 1/4 green onion
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 3/4 cup pasta (rigatoni or penne recommended)
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • basil (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup parmesan
Method
  1. Put the onion, garlic and olive oil in a large saucepan and cook until soft
  2. Add in the heavy cream and vodka and cook through until evaporated
  3. Add red pepper flakes (generously if you want the pasta to be spicier)
  4. Stir until combined and season with salt and pepper
  5. Remove sauce from the heat
  6. Cook pasta in a separate pan and before draining, save ¼ cup of the pasta water
  7. Add in butter to the sauce and stir until it melts over medium heat
  8. Put cooked pasta and pasta water into saucepan
  9. Sprinkle basil and parmesan on top

Steak Fajitas

Overview: These steak fajitas are super tasty and easy to make if you buy Trader Joe's carne asada. You can also customize them to your liking with whatever toppings and sides you like.

Ingredients
  • Trader Joe’s Carne Asada
  • Mini Corn or flour tortillas
  • ½ Yellow onion
  • ½ Red pepper
  • ½ tablespoon Olive Oil
  • Sour cream (optional)
Method
  1. Heat medium skillet and cook the carne asada according to the directions on the package
  2. While the meat is cooking, slice up the vegetables into medium sized pieces
  3. Remove meat when it is done cooking and put it to the side
  4. Oil the same pan the meat was cooking in and cook the vegetables
  5. Place tortillas in the oven or toaster oven to warm up
  6. Assemble the fajitas and serve with sour cream
